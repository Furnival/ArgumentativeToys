Shared Bus, Shared Attack Surface

An argumentative toy about housekeeping as a side-channel attack

A hosted payload is supposed to sit behind a boundary: your sensor, my sensor, one spacecraft bus, no peeking through the wall. But the bus still reports its own health — where it is pointed, how much power is being drawn, what is heating up, and how regularly its state changes. Each channel looks harmless alone. This toy argues that on a multi-tenant satellite, those ordinary housekeeping signals can combine into a side channel that reveals what another tenant is doing, even when the payload data stays encrypted.

No housekeeping field says much alone; together, they reveal the tenant’s intent.

This is a toy, not a simulation. It reduces a hosted payload interface to four simplified observables — pointing, power, heat, and timing — to show how weak signals combine into a side channel, not to model any one spacecraft exactly.
